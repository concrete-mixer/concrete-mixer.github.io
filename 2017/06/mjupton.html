New sounds by Michael Upton

June 11, 2017

Michael is a friend of mine who lives up the road and (in my view at least) is NZ’s leading electronica practitioner.

Michael’s recorded all over the place, and has generously donated several sounds to the cause.

Contributions

Lake Tarawera Dam

This recording features NZ birdlife both native and introduced recorded at Lake Tarawera. Intriguingly, in the background there’s occasionally something that sounds like a sliding door opening or closing.

Cadiz Cathedral parts 1 and 2

Oddly the CM collection already features a recording of Cadiz Cathedral from 2011, when the cathedral was under renovation and I captured the sound of a power drill reverberating majestically. This more intimate recording (“curated” into two separate segments for CM) features an excellent tight echo from an alcove or chapel or script or some other chamber.

Rissington Dam

This recording features some nice interplay from birdlife and some amphibian creaking.

Taken from the start of the same source recording, here’s the sound of Michael’s jandals as he walks away from the recorder. It’s an iconic kiwi sound, like buzzy bees and hokey pokey ice cream, mother fuckers!

Fireworks in Karori parts 1 and 2

This Guy Fawke’s night recording features a (literally) sparkling performance by some fireworks, but also provides a spring suburban soundscape. With some cameo dialogue from some classic kiwi munters, this recording has it all!

Train and train station (Ella, Sri Lanka)

Again I’ve taken two fragments from a single recording. The first features the ambience of a Sri Lankan train station, while the second has a train passing by. The aural climax is a flash of doppler flange at the moment the engine passes. Magic!

Car wash, Levin

It turns out, interestingly, if you listen to the sounds of a car wash when you’re not actually in the car as it is being washed, they sound remarkably like a granular synthesis-driven piece of sound art.

As we kiwis say, thanks Moykull!
